Types of Sihr (Magic) - Comprehensive Guide

Understanding the different manifestations of black magic as described in Islamic sources

Quick Access

Sihr of Separation (Sihr Al-Tafriq)

↑

Purpose

To create discord and separation between two people, often spouses or loved ones.

Effects

Causes hatred, anger, misunderstandings, and emotional distance between individuals. Sudden unexplained conflicts arise between people who previously had good relationships.

Common Signs

  • • Married couples suddenly despising each other without cause
  • • Family members becoming enemies overnight
  • • Business partners breaking apart in anger
  • • Close friends developing unexplained hatred

Quranic Reference

This type of sihr is implied in Surah Al-Falaq (113:4): "And from the evil of those who blow on knots." Regular recitation of Al-Falaq and An-Nas provides protection.

Sihr of Love or Attraction (Sihr Al-Mahabbah)

↑

Purpose

To force someone to fall in love with or become obsessed with another person against their will.

Effects

The victim may feel an unnatural attraction, obsession, or compulsion toward someone, even if they are otherwise incompatible or disinterested. They may abandon their values or previous relationships.

Common Signs

  • • Sudden intense attraction to someone inappropriate
  • • Ignoring obvious character flaws in the person
  • • Abandoning family or friends for this person
  • • Obsessive thoughts that cannot be controlled

Warning

This type of sihr is often used for manipulation and violates the free will of the individual. It can destroy marriages and families, leading people into haram relationships.

Sihr of Harm or Destruction (Sihr Al-Adh'a)

↑

Purpose

To cause physical or mental harm to a person through supernatural means.

Effects

Can manifest as chronic illnesses, unexplained pain, fatigue, depression, anxiety, or other psychological and physiological conditions that have no apparent medical cause.

Common Symptoms

  • • Chronic pain that doctors cannot diagnose
  • • Severe depression or anxiety without cause
  • • Extreme fatigue and weakness
  • • Recurring nightmares and sleep disturbances
  • • Physical symptoms that worsen during prayer

Quranic Protection

Surah Al-Falaq and Surah An-Nas are recited for protection against such harm. Regular ruqyah and maintaining spiritual cleanliness provide the strongest defense.

Sihr of Confusion or Madness (Sihr Al-Hayal)

↑

Purpose

To confuse, disorient, or drive someone insane through supernatural interference with their mental faculties.

Effects

Victims may experience hallucinations, memory loss, irrational behavior, or a distorted perception of reality. They may become unable to think clearly or make rational decisions.

Common Signs

  • • Seeing or hearing things that aren't there
  • • Severe memory problems and confusion
  • • Irrational fears and paranoia
  • • Speaking incoherently or to invisible entities
  • • Inability to distinguish reality from imagination

Significance

This type of sihr is often associated with jinn possession or demonic influence. It requires intensive ruqyah treatment and may need professional Islamic healing.

Sihr of Loss or Misplacement (Sihr Al-Khasf)

↑

Purpose

To cause someone to lose valuable items, wealth, or opportunities through supernatural interference.

Effects

Results in forgetfulness, misplacement of belongings, financial losses, or missed chances in life. The person becomes prone to accidents and poor decision-making.

Common Manifestations

  • • Constantly losing important documents or items
  • • Missing crucial appointments or opportunities
  • • Making poor financial decisions repeatedly
  • • Forgetting where money or valuables are placed
  • • Bad luck in investments or business ventures

Protection

Regular recitation of Ayat al-Kursi and the three Quls (Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas) provides protection. Maintaining regular dhikr and seeking Allah's guidance in decisions helps counter this sihr.

Sihr of Physical Transformation (Sihr At-Taswir)

↑

Purpose

To alter a person's appearance or physical state through supernatural means.

Effects

May cause physical deformities, premature aging, sudden weight gain or loss, or changes in facial features. This type of sihr is less common but mentioned in traditional Islamic sources.

Possible Manifestations

  • • Sudden unexplained changes in appearance
  • • Rapid aging or deterioration of looks
  • • Skin conditions that don't respond to treatment
  • • Dramatic weight changes without medical cause
  • • Hair loss or changes in hair texture

Important Note

This type of sihr is rare and should not be the first assumption for physical changes. Medical causes should always be investigated first before considering supernatural explanations.

Treatment

Consistent ruqyah treatment combined with medical care when appropriate. Trust in Allah's decree while seeking both spiritual and physical healing.

Sihr of Speech or Tongue (Sihr Al-Lisan)

↑

Purpose

To prevent someone from speaking the truth, defending themselves, or expressing their thoughts clearly.

Effects

Causes stuttering, inability to articulate words properly, or being consistently misunderstood when speaking. The person may feel tongue-tied in important situations.

Common Signs

  • • Sudden speech problems in crucial moments
  • • Words coming out differently than intended
  • • Inability to defend oneself verbally
  • • Stuttering or stammering in important conversations
  • • People consistently misunderstanding what you say

Timing Patterns

Often occurs during job interviews, court appearances, important presentations, or when trying to resolve conflicts. The speech problems may be temporary but appear at crucial moments.

Protection

Recite "Rabbish-rahli sadri wa yassirli amri wahlul uqdatan min lisani yafqahu qawli" (My Lord, expand my chest and ease my task and untie the knot from my tongue that they may understand my speech) - Quran 20:25-28.

Sihr of Sleep or Dreams (Sihr An-Naum)

↑

Purpose

To disturb a person's sleep or manipulate their dreams to cause psychological distress and weaken their mental state.

Effects

Victims may experience chronic insomnia, recurring nightmares, or disturbing dreams. This sihr can be used to weaken a person's mental state over time through sleep deprivation.

Common Symptoms

  • • Inability to fall asleep despite being tired
  • • Recurring nightmares with similar themes
  • • Waking up exhausted despite sleeping
  • • Sleep paralysis or feeling attacked in dreams
  • • Dreams that feel unusually real and disturbing

Dream Content

Dreams may involve frightening scenarios, deceased people, snakes, or sexual content designed to disturb the person's peace of mind and spiritual state.

Protection

Recite Ayat al-Kursi before sleep, the three Quls, and maintain wudu when sleeping. Keep the sleeping area clean and play Quran audio softly during sleep if needed.

Sihr of Pregnancy Prevention (Sihr Al-Man'i lil-Haml)

↑

Purpose

To prevent a woman from conceiving or carrying a pregnancy to term through supernatural interference.

Effects

Causes unexplained infertility, repeated miscarriages, or complications during pregnancy despite being medically healthy.

Common Signs

  • • Inability to conceive despite medical clearance
  • • Repeated early miscarriages without medical cause
  • • Pregnancy complications that doctors cannot explain
  • • Dreams of losing babies or being attacked while pregnant
  • • Feeling unnatural aversion to the idea of pregnancy

Important Considerations

Always seek proper medical evaluation first. Many fertility issues have medical causes that can be treated. Spiritual treatment should complement, not replace, medical care.

Spiritual Treatment

Regular ruqyah, especially focusing on reproductive health, drinking ruqyah water, and making specific du'a for children. Remember that Allah has ultimate control over all matters of conception and birth.

Sihr of Wealth or Prosperity (Sihr Al-Ghina)

↑

Purpose

To block someone from gaining wealth or prosperity, keeping them in perpetual financial hardship.

Effects

Leads to persistent financial struggles, job losses, failed businesses, or an inability to save money despite sincere efforts and hard work.

Common Patterns

  • • Businesses failing just as they start to succeed
  • • Unexpected expenses consuming all savings
  • • Job opportunities disappearing at the last moment
  • • Investments consistently performing poorly
  • • Money seeming to disappear despite careful budgeting

Recognition Signs

Financial problems that persist despite genuine effort, good skills, and favorable market conditions. The pattern of near-success followed by sudden failure is often characteristic.

Islamic Response

Regular recitation of Surah Al-Waqiah and Surah Yasin, seeking halal income, giving charity regularly, and making du'a for barakah in rizq. Trust that Allah is the ultimate provider while taking proper worldly means.

Sihr of Death (Sihr Al-Qatl)

↑

Purpose

To cause someone's death through magical means - the most severe and dangerous type of sihr.

Effects

Often linked to severe illnesses that don't respond to treatment, mysterious accidents, or sudden deaths without clear medical explanation. May cause gradual deterioration of health.

Warning Signs

  • • Rapid decline in health without medical cause
  • • Multiple organ systems failing simultaneously
  • • Treatments that work for others failing consistently
  • • Premonitions of death or threatening dreams
  • • Feeling of impending doom without logical reason

Islamic Perspective

No one dies except by Allah's permission at a predetermined time. However, sihr can be a means through which Allah's decree is carried out, just as illness or accidents can be.

Protection and Response

Intensive ruqyah treatment, seeking medical help, increasing dhikr and Quran recitation, repentance (tawbah), charity, and complete trust in Allah's protection. Remember that life and death are in Allah's hands alone.

Sihr of Binding (Sihr Al-'Uqad)

↑

Purpose

To bind or restrict someone's abilities, actions, or decisions, making them feel paralyzed or unable to act.

Effects

Makes the victim feel paralyzed, indecisive, or unable to take action, even in situations where they should have control. They become passive and unable to assert themselves.

Common Manifestations

  • • Inability to make important life decisions
  • • Feeling paralyzed when action is needed
  • • Procrastination in critical matters
  • • Feeling controlled or manipulated by others
  • • Loss of personal willpower and initiative

Physical Aspects

This sihr often involves literal knots (mentioned in Surah Al-Falaq) that are tied while reciting spells. The spiritual binding mirrors physical knots.

Breaking the Binding

Surah Al-Falaq is specifically revealed for this type of sihr. Regular recitation, seeking Allah's help to break all spiritual bonds, and gradually taking small actions to rebuild confidence and willpower.

Sihr of Jinn Summoning or Possession (Sihr Al-Jinniyyah)

↑

Purpose

To summon or enslave jinn to carry out harmful acts on behalf of the sorcerer, including possessing the victim.

Effects

Jinn may possess the victim, causing physical ailments, mental disturbances, erratic behavior, or supernatural phenomena around the person.

Signs of Jinn Involvement

  • • Dramatic personality changes
  • • Speaking in unknown languages or different voices
  • • Violent reactions to Quran recitation
  • • Supernatural strength or knowledge
  • • Aversion to prayer and Islamic practices
  • • Strange occurrences in the environment

Complexity

This is often the most complex type of sihr as it involves direct jinn involvement. Treatment may take longer and require experienced Islamic healing practitioners.

Protection

Reciting Ayat Al-Kursi (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:255), Surah Al-Ikhlas, Surah Al-Falaq, and Surah An-Nas provides protection. Consistent ruqyah, maintaining wudu, and staying in spiritually clean environments are essential.

Waswas al-Shaytan (Waswas [Whispers])

↑

Purpose

To implant persistent negative thoughts, create doubts about faith, or cause obsessive-compulsive behaviors that interfere with worship and daily life.

Effects

Persistent negative thoughts, doubts about faith, or obsessions (OCD-like symptoms). The victim experiences intrusive thoughts that are difficult to control or dismiss.

Common Manifestations

  • • Constant doubts about Islamic beliefs or practices
  • • Obsessive thoughts about cleanliness affecting prayer
  • • Repetitive negative thoughts about oneself or others
  • • Compulsive behaviors that interfere with worship
  • • Persistent blasphemous thoughts despite strong faith

Important Distinction

Normal waswas from shaytan is different from sihr-induced waswas. Sihr-based whispers are more persistent, intense, and interfere significantly with daily functioning and worship.

Islamic Treatment

Seek refuge in Allah (A'udhu billahi min ash-shaytani'r-rajim), ignore the whispers, increase dhikr, and remember that having such thoughts is not sinful - acting upon them is. Consistent ruqyah helps break the pattern.

Sihr al-Intihār / Sihr al-Huzn (Self-Harm Magic / Depression)

↑

Purpose

To induce severe depression, feelings of worthlessness, and potentially suicidal thoughts as a means of causing ultimate harm to the victim.

Effects

Leads to feelings of worthlessness, suicidal thoughts, and long-term sadness without external cause. The depression feels overwhelming and disproportionate to life circumstances.

Warning Signs

  • • Sudden onset of severe depression without cause
  • • Persistent thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • • Feeling completely worthless despite achievements
  • • Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities
  • • Isolation from family, friends, and community

Critical Warning

If experiencing suicidal thoughts, seek immediate professional help alongside spiritual treatment. Contact mental health services, trusted family members, or religious counselors immediately.

Treatment Approach

Combine ruqyah with professional mental health support. Remember that life is sacred in Islam, increase connection with Allah through prayer, surround yourself with supportive believers, and maintain hope in Allah's mercy.

Sihr al-Ighra' (Addiction or Obsessive Cravings)

↑

Purpose

To create intense, irrational cravings for harmful substances or behaviors, leading the person into sin and self-destruction.

Effects

Causes intense, irrational craving for something harmful such as drugs, alcohol, pornography, zina, or haram relationships. The cravings feel uncontrollable and overwhelming.

Common Targets

  • • Substance abuse (drugs, alcohol, cigarettes)
  • • Sexual addictions and inappropriate relationships
  • • Gambling and financial recklessness
  • • Food addictions leading to health problems
  • • Technology or social media obsessions

Recognition Signs

Sudden onset of intense cravings for things previously not problematic, inability to control urges despite religious convictions, and continued behavior despite negative consequences.

Breaking Free

Intensive ruqyah focusing on breaking spiritual bonds, seeking professional addiction counseling, removing triggers from environment, increasing Islamic practices, and finding supportive Islamic community for accountability.

Sihr al-Maradh (Sihr of Sickness)

↑

Purpose

To cause chronic illness and physical suffering that cannot be diagnosed or treated through conventional medical means.

Effects

Victim suffers from unexplained illness despite medical tests being normal. Symptoms persist or worsen without clear medical cause or effective treatment response.

Characteristic Patterns

  • • Symptoms that medical tests cannot explain
  • • Treatments that work for others fail consistently
  • • Symptoms worsen during religious activities
  • • Multiple body systems affected simultaneously
  • • Illness follows specific patterns or timing

Medical Approach

Always pursue proper medical diagnosis and treatment first. Spiritual healing should complement, not replace, medical care. Some conditions may have both spiritual and physical components.

Spiritual Healing

Regular ruqyah, drinking ruqyah water, using ruqyah oil on affected areas, maintaining regular prayers despite illness, and having complete trust in Allah's decree while seeking all available means of healing.

Sihr al-Nisyān (Sihr of Forgetfulness / Laziness)

↑

Purpose

To create mental laziness and forgetfulness specifically targeting religious knowledge and practices, weakening the person's spiritual connection.

Effects

Causes mental laziness, failure to remember Quran or concentrate during prayer. The person may feel mentally foggy specifically during religious activities while remaining sharp in worldly matters.

Specific Symptoms

  • • Forgetting verses of Quran previously memorized
  • • Inability to concentrate during prayer
  • • Mental fog when trying to learn Islamic knowledge
  • • Excessive sleepiness during religious activities
  • • Procrastination specifically in religious duties
  • • Clear thinking in worldly matters but confusion in religious ones

Pattern Recognition

The forgetfulness and laziness are selective - affecting religious matters more than worldly concerns. This distinguishes it from general memory problems or laziness.

Spiritual Counter-Measures

Regular ruqyah before religious activities, asking Allah to remove mental barriers, starting with small amounts of worship and gradually increasing, seeking Allah's help to remember and concentrate, and being patient with the healing process.

Important Reminder

This guide is for educational purposes only to help identify potential spiritual afflictions. Always consult qualified Islamic scholars and medical professionals for proper diagnosis and treatment. Not every problem is caused by sihr - many issues have natural causes that should be investigated first. Remember that Allah is the ultimate healer and protector.
