What is Ruqyah?

Understanding Islamic spiritual healing through Quranic verses and prophetic supplications

Quick Access

What is Ruqyah?

↑

Ruqyah is the Islamic practice of spiritual healing through recitation of Quranic verses and supplications (du'a) from the Sunnah.

Definition

Ruqyah comes from the Arabic root meaning "to blow" or "to recite over." It involves reciting specific verses while seeking Allah's protection and healing.

Purpose

• Seeking protection from evil eye, jinn, and sihr (magic) • Spiritual healing and purification • Strengthening faith and connection to Allah

Prophetic Foundation

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever among you is able to benefit his brother, let him do so." (Muslim)

Why This Works

The jinn are extremely sensitive to dhikr and ruqyah — they try to suppress your consciousness to avoid being detected. But when you actively push back, they weaken and flee. Even whispering dhikr inside your heart burns them and disrupts their influence.

Types of Ruqyah

↑
✓ Ruqyah Shar'iyyah (Lawful Ruqyah)

• Uses only Quran and authentic Prophetic du'a • Reciter maintains pure belief in Allah's power • No shirk (associating partners with Allah)

This is the only type of ruqyah Muslims should practice

✗ Ruqyah Shirkiyyah (Unlawful Ruqyah)

• Uses unknown words, talismans, or charms • Involves seeking help from jinn or spirits • Contains elements of shirk or superstition

This type is forbidden and dangerous

What Makes Ruqyah Valid?

1. Must be in Arabic or a known language 2. Must not contain shirk or forbidden elements 3. Belief that Allah alone provides the cure

How to Perform Ruqyah

↑
Self Ruqyah (Recommended)

• Be in wudu and face qiblah if possible • Recite with conviction and concentration • Place hand on area of discomfort • Blow gently after recitation

Ruqyah for Others

• Only by trustworthy, knowledgeable Muslims • Patient should be modestly dressed • Avoid inappropriate touching • Family member should be present

Best Times

• After Fajr and before sunrise • Between Maghrib and Isha • After any obligatory prayer • During the last third of the night

Essential Verses

• Al-Fatiha • Ayat al-Kursi (2:255) • Last two verses of Al-Baqarah • Surah Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas

Ruqyah Verses

A single verse or du'ā can be a valid ruqyah, such as:

• Surah al-Fātiḥah (even alone, it's considered a powerful ruqyah)

• Āyat al-Kursī (Surah al-Baqarah 2:255)

• The last two verses of Surah al-Baqarah

• Surah al-Ikhlāṣ, al-Falaq, and al-Nās

The Prophet ﷺ himself used brief ruqyahs such as:

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ

"Aʿūdhu bi kalimātillāhi al-tāmmāti min sharri mā khalaq."

(I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah from the evil of what He has created.)

— Sahih Muslim, Book 26, Hadith 5444

Signs & Symptoms During Ruqyah

↑
Positive Reactions

• Feeling calm and peaceful • Increased focus during recitation • Relief from anxiety or depression • Better sleep and appetite

Possible Reactions (Normal)

• Yawning, drowsiness, or crying • Temporary headache or nausea • Feeling heat or cold in body • Emotional release or memories surfacing

Strong Reactions (May Indicate Affliction)

• Severe agitation or anger • Fainting or seizure-like movements • Speaking in unknown languages • Aversion to Quranic recitation

Important Notes

• Reactions don't always indicate jinn possession • Some people are naturally more sensitive • Continue with regular ruqyah regardless of reactions • Seek knowledgeable help for severe cases

Conditions Treated by Ruqyah

↑
Evil Eye (Ayn/Hasad)

• Sudden illness after praise or admiration • Unexplained fatigue or loss of blessings • Family or business problems • Feeling of being "watched"

Jinn Possession/Interference

• Hearing voices or seeing things others don't • Unexplained behavioral changes • Aversion to prayer or Quran • Sleep paralysis or nightmares

Sihr (Black Magic/Witchcraft)

• Sudden personality changes • Unexplained hatred between spouses • Chronic unexplained illnesses • Finding strange objects in home

Important Reminder

Always seek medical treatment alongside spiritual healing. Allah has provided both spiritual and physical means of cure.

Daily Protection Through Ruqyah

↑
Morning Protection

• Read Ayat al-Kursi after Fajr • Recite the three Quls (3x each) • Morning adhkar and du'a • Blow over palms and wipe over body

Evening Protection

• Evening adhkar after Maghrib • Recite Surah Al-Baqarah in home • Sleep with recitation of last verses of Al-Baqarah • Keep wudu before sleep

Home Protection

• Play Surah Al-Baqarah regularly • Keep home clean and free of images • Regular dhikr and prayer • Avoid music and inappropriate content

Emergency Protection

• A'udhu billahi min ash-shaytani'r-rajim • Ayat al-Kursi • Last two verses of Al-Baqarah • Trust in Allah and remain steadfast

Remember

Ruqyah is a blessed practice that strengthens your connection with Allah and provides spiritual protection. Practice it regularly as part of your daily routine, and remember that healing comes from Allah alone. Combine spiritual remedies with proper medical care when needed.
